Here are 4 ways to differentiate "AlphaFilter" and make the judges remember your project:
1. The "Signal Decay" Metric (The Quant USP)
What others will do: "Apple had good news today, so sentiment is Positive."
Your USP: News matters less as time passes. If a great earnings report came out 48 hours ago, the "alpha" (the edge) is gone because the market has already priced it in.
How to build it: Introduce a "Signal Decay" metric. If news broke 10 minutes ago, score is 95/100. If 2 days ago, it decays to 10/100.
2. The "Contrarian / Divergence" Alert (The Trader USP)
What others will do: "Sentiment is bad, so don't buy."
Your USP: The best traders look for divergences‚Äîwhen the stock price does the opposite of the news (e.g., terrible news but price doesn't drop implies strong support).
How to build it: Compare trailing 3-day Price Action with 3-day Sentiment Score. Flag "Teflon Stocks" (price UP, sentiment DOWN) or "Value Traps" (price DOWN, sentiment UP).
3. "Entity Extraction" & Supply Chain Contagion (The Institutional USP)
What others will do: Analyze news only about the target ticker (e.g., TSLA).
Your USP: A sophisticated tool understands supply chain contagion. If a lithium mine burns down, it affects Tesla.
How to build it: Use an LLM to map a target's top 3 suppliers/competitors and pull news for those entities as well.
